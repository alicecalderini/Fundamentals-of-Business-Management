# Cost Foresight – Forecasting Production Costs in the Steel Industry

## Project Overview

This project was developed for the **Fundamentals of Business Management** course.

The objective is to design a **predictive framework to forecast production costs in the steel industry**, a sector where costs are highly volatile due to fluctuations in energy prices and raw material markets.

The project integrates **simulated accounting data** with **market indices** to explore how data-driven models can support managerial decision-making.

---

## Problem

Steel manufacturing companies face significant uncertainty in production costs due to:

* Volatile **energy prices**
* Fluctuations in **raw material costs**
* Complex relationships between production efficiency and accounting metrics

These factors make **cost planning and forecasting difficult** for management.

---

## Project Application

The project proposes a **predictive model prototype** that combines:

* Simulated accounting data
* External market indicators
* Statistical forecasting techniques

The goal is to **estimate future production costs and support strategic planning**.

---

## Accounting Framework

The analysis applies several accounting and performance metrics:

* **Gross Margin Trend Analysis**
* **COGS Ratio by cost center**
* **Inventory Turnover**

These indicators help connect **production cost forecasts with asset utilization and operational efficiency**.

---

## Academic Scope

The project focuses on **analytical and conceptual modeling**, suitable for academic research.

Included methods:

* Regression models
* Time series forecasting

Excluded:

* Industrial-scale simulations
* Real corporate financial systems
